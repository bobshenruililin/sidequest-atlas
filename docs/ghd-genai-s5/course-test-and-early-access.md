## What they are testing (decoded)

This is not a “who knows GLP-1 best” quiz. From the tutor guide + worksheet design, the session scores **process literacy**, not biomedical depth.

| Signal they want | How the activity forces it |
|------------------|----------------------------|
| **Prompt craft as research skill** | Same three prompts × three models; Prompt 2 deliberately stresses RCT claims |
| **Hallucination detection** | Pre-reading on fabricated citations (Chelli 2024); students must verify PMIDs / DOIs |
| **Source evaluation (CRAAP)** | Consensus scoring before any “answer” |
| **Human synthesis without AI** | Explicit rule: team summary **without** GenAI |
| **Interdisciplinary collaboration** | Designed BBMS:GHD mix; GHD brings population / equity framing |
| **Uncertainty honesty** | Prompt 3 asks limitations; CRAAP Accuracy rewards caveats |
| **Responsible use stance** | Aligns with Cochrane AI position + JAMA “machines ≠ authors” |

**What they are *not* testing hard:** memorised pharmacology, perfect epidemiology methods, or “pick the smartest chatbot.”

**Grade reality (tutor guide):** participation, not a high-stakes mark. The *real* assessment is whether you look competent in front of BBMS peers and tutors when citations fail.

## Early-access advantage (if you stay in GHD)

You already completed a full RQ4 dry run with three models, CRAAP consensus, citation checks, and a human team summary. When Session 5 runs for real:

1. **Role clarity:** Offer to be the **citation auditor** (PMID/DOI checks) and the **population-health framer** (risk difference ≠ absolute risk; confounding; equity). Let BBMS peers lean into molecular / trial detail.
2. **Speed:** You know Prompt 2 is the trap. Lead the group to verify *every* PMID before scoring Accuracy.
3. **Facilitator language:** You can name the learning goal out loud (“we’re testing verification, not model branding”) — that reads as senior even in Year 1.
4. **Feedback capital:** Rex invited your perspective. A crisp, constructive email after the test run builds a coordinator relationship inside MedFaculty AI literacy — useful if GHD stays your lane.
5. **Portfolio artifact:** Keep the worksheet + reflection in Sidequest Atlas (this folder). It is evidence of AI-literacy practice aligned to faculty standards, not vibes.

### What *not* to do with early access

- Do not paste this dry-run answer as the group’s live product (collaboration + their own model runs matter).
- Do not overclaim clinical authority on GLP-1 / gallstones in front of BBMS — own the **methods and verification** lane.
- Do not treat HKU ChatGPT portal outputs as identical to this Cursor proxy run; re-run live on `https://chatgpt.hku.hk/` in class.

## Cutting-edge check (blunt)

The school is **not** inventing frontier AI science here. The stack is familiar 2023–2025 literacy: CRAAP, hallucination papers, “don’t let AI write the conclusion,” compare vendors.

That is fine. GHD value is not being more cutting-edge than Stanford ML — it is being **earlier and cleaner than peers** at:

- refusing invented citations,
- separating association from causation,
- writing a human synthesis that a tutor can trust.

Your dry run already practices that. The course will feel easier than a cold start; use the spare cognitive load to **teach the group** and look collaborative, not showy.
