Thank you for the warm introduction and for inviting me to test Session 5. I completed a dry run of **Research Question 4** (GLP-1RA use and gallstone risk among people with obesity), following the student prompts as closely as possible.

**Quick answers to your feedback questions**

1. **Interesting?** Yes. Comparing three models on the *same* prompts made the differences in tone, citation behaviour, and hedging very concrete — more useful than a lecture on “AI can hallucinate.”

2. **Feasible within the instructions?** Mostly yes for a prepared individual dry run. In a live 110-minute tutorial with four students, it should be feasible **if** groups divide labour early (one person per model for Prompt 1–2, shared Prompt 3, then CRAAP + human summary). Without that split, three full model passes plus verification will feel tight.

3. **Unclear parts?** A few:
   - The student CRAAP table totals **/20** (four criteria), while the tutor guide also discusses **Purpose** — students may wonder whether Purpose is scored.
   - “Copy the entire output” is clear; it would help to say whether screenshots are acceptable if the portal truncates long replies.
   - Prompt 2 asks models to “state clearly if no RCTs exist,” but some models still invent trial-like citations — a one-line tutor tip (“verify every PMID before scoring Accuracy”) would save groups time.
   - For mixed GHD/BBMS groups, a sentence on **expected GHD contribution** (population framing, equity, confounding) would reduce anxiety that BBMS peers “own” the biomedical content.

4. **Too difficult for junior GHD students?** The **biomedical topic** (GLP-1RA, cholelithiasis) will feel intimidating at first glance, but the *assessed skills* (prompting, CRAAP, citation checks, human synthesis) are appropriate for Year 1 if tutors emphasise that medical detail is scaffolding, not the grading target. RQ4 is harder than a methods-only question; pairing with BBMS helps, and GHD students can lead on uncertainty and population interpretation.

5. **Other suggestions**
   - Add a 5-minute “PMID verification drill” before scoring.
   - Clarify whether students must use only `chatgpt.hku.hk` engines or may compare an external model for contrast.
   - Align student CRAAP columns with the tutor guide (include or explicitly drop Purpose).
   - Optional stretch: ask groups for one sentence on **how the answer would change for a public-health vs clinical audience**.

I am happy to share my filled worksheet and a short reflection if useful for the BBMS parallel test run. I used three distinct model backends for the comparison; in class I would of course follow the HKU portal engines named in the live instructions.

Thank you again for developing this for MedFaculty students — the activity is already in good shape, and the tweaks above are polish rather than redesign.
