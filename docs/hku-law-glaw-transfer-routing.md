## What happened

Bob emailed **ASO** (`transfer@hku.hk`) for internal transfer from BASc(GHAD) Year 1.

**Wording problem (primary):**  
Desired curriculum stated as **LLB Year 1**, then:  
“I would also be grateful to be considered for BSocSc(Government and Laws) & LLB, Year 1, **if appropriate**.”

That is a **soft secondary / optional ask**, not an unambiguous dual application with ranked choices. Administrators reading fast will treat **LLB as the application** and GLaws as a polite maybe.

**Expected consequence:** ASO forwards to Faculty of Law → Law UG Admissions emails for documents (what Bob received). Silence from Social Sciences / PPA does **not** equal rejection; it equals **routing not confirmed**.

HKU process (public guidance): one email to ASO with desired curriculum + reasons + supporting docs; faculties have their own deadlines; ASO routes to relevant units.

## LLB vs GLaws for *this* Bob

| | **LLB** | **GLaws** BSocSc(Govt&Laws)&LLB |
|---|---------|----------------------------------|
| Length | ~4 years | ~5 years (BSocSc exit possible after Y2 path under regs — confirm current) |
| Policy training | Self-assembled (electives / minor) | Built-in PPA major + research/internship-style capstone signals |
| Legal qualification path | LLB → PCLL track (PCLL still competitive/separate) | Same LLB academic foundation if five-year track completed |
| Internships / opportunities | Law Faculty network, clinics, moots | Law **plus** PPA/GLaws cohort, alumni AO/policy pipeline, advertised career scaffolding |
| Fit to GHAD → CHP / regulation / China CDC story | Possible if he builds policy deliberately | **Closer intellectual fit** to institutions, governance, public affairs |
| Cost | Faster finish; more free-elective room | Extra year; denser prescribed curriculum |

**Blunt recommendation if policy + internships are genuine drivers:** prefer **GLaws first, LLB second** — and say that consistently in every email.  
**Keep LLB first** only if the goal is the shortest path to a qualifying LLB with maximum elective flexibility.

GLaws does **not** guarantee better internships; it improves infrastructure and narrative fit. Grades, writing, relationships still decide placements.

**JD later:** if he transfers into LLB/GLaws now, a later JD is usually not the path to *basic* legal qualification. The old “GHAD then JD” plan is an alternative to transfer, not a stack on top of an LLB.

- **Charitable read:** interest in law *and* public affairs.  
- **Risk read:** LLB is the real goal; GLaws is an unresearched backup; “if appropriate” outsources the choice.  
- **Clean sentence:**  
  “My objective is legal and institutional competence at the intersection of public health, regulation, and public policy; my preference order is [1], then [2].”

Do **not** tell admissions the offer is “reversible,” that medicine closed, or that this is scholarship optionality. Internally fine; externally it reads as uncommitted.
