# Philosophy

Travel is fieldwork, not checklist completion.

1. Ordinary life is a destination — supermarkets, campuses, ferries, worker lunches, housing, transit.
2. Cheap, intentional travel removes insulation.
3. Frugality is not deprivation. Avoid premium prices for mediocre convenience.
4. Money is an unlocking mechanism. Spend when an experience is rare, seasonal, culturally specific, personally meaningful, or truly excellent.
5. A coherent day beats a collection of attractions.
6. Leave room for reality to interrupt.
7. Food is cultural fieldwork.
8. Universities reveal the future of a place.
9. Companies and institutions reveal how a society organizes capital, talent, trust, technology, and ambition.
10. Sidequests create stories.
11. Every trip should produce a personal thesis.

Spending rule: **Expensive can be worthwhile. Overpriced is not.**

Daily rhythm when appropriate: live like a student in the morning, eat like a worker at lunch, and occasionally dine like a king at night.
