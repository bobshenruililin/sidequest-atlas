## English opening (~90 seconds)

Good afternoon. My name is Shen Ruililin — Bob. I was born in Shanghai and raised in Hong Kong; I do not see those identities as competing. My grandparents were farmers and industrial workers, so I start from ordinary lives, illness, trust, and dignity.

The question I want to pursue is: how can Hong Kong help make China’s public-health capacity trusted and interoperable for an ageing world?

In Wudu I listened to retired steelworkers; I also saw a Tibetan temple that functioned as a medical centre. Across systems, trust is often the first thing to break. At Shenzhen Bay in 2020 I saw the seam between Hong Kong’s common-law health governance and Guangdong’s CDC system. That seam is where I intend to work.

At HKU in Global Health and Development, my Laidlaw research with Professor Bishai studies heat and cardiovascular admissions using Hospital Authority data from around 2013–2023, aiming toward dual-season hospital preparedness and interoperable surveillance. Long term I hope to serve in a senior policy role at the Centre for Health Protection on cross-boundary disease control. If I later pursue a JD, it would be after undergraduate study — regulatory and common-law training for health policy, not a departure from public health.

I am applying because this Foundation’s mission matches the person I am trying to become: rooted in Hong Kong, culturally serious about China, willing to work in the GBA, and oriented to public service.

各位教授、各位評審，午安。我係沈睿霖，大家可以叫我 Bob。  
我喺上海出世，喺香港長大；對我嚟講，呢兩個身份唔係互相競爭。

我而家最想追問：香港點樣可以幫中國嘅公共衞生能力，變得更加可信、更加可以互通，去回應一個老齡化嘅世界？  
喺武都同退休鋼鐵工人傾偈，亦見過同時有醫療功能嘅寺院——跨制度合作入面，最容易斷裂嘅往往係信任。  
2020 年喺深圳灣口岸，我感受到香港普通法公共衞生治理同廣東疾控系統之間嘅交界；呢條「縫」就係我想工作嘅地方。  
我喺港大讀 Global Health and Development；Laidlaw 研究同 Bishai 教授睇高溫同心血管入院，思考雙季醫院準備。  
長遠希望喺衞生防護中心做跨境疾控政策；如果將來讀 JD，係本科之後，作為監管同普通法訓練，唔係離開公共衞生。多謝。
