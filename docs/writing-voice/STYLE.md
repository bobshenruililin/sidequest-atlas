## Core identity in prose

Bob writes as a **translator between systems**: data ↔ lived life; lab ↔ field; East ↔ West; design ↔ delivery; Hong Kong ↔ Mainland / Global South. Recurring self-definition: *bridge*, *pracademic*, *structural fluency*, *messy middle*, *signal loss* between story and lever.

Career north star (stable across samples): **policy-focused epidemiology / urban health policy** — environmental and climate data → actionable protection for vulnerable populations (esp. aging, high-density cities). Research is framed as **prerequisite for service**, not prestige.

## What “good Bob writing” does

1. **Opens in a scene or object**, then names the thesis. Berlin plenary gap; camera as passport; grandmother’s lane with 拆; woman measuring the road to Lhasa with her body; basketball pick-and-roll; calligraphy irreversibility.
2. **Runs on productive dualities** without becoming a slogan farm: invention/witnessing; abstract/intervention; court/brush; economist’s logic / anthropologist’s conscience.
3. **Claims leadership as connection and translation**, not title collection. Ambassadorship, facilitation, documentation, “raising the standard without raising the temperature.”
4. **Allows honesty and friction** when the genre permits (St John’s integration: fractured presence, room repurposed, grace via floor chairs). Vulnerability is specific, not performative confession.
5. **Ends with a short, load-bearing line** — almost aphoristic: *I am ready to be a Laidlaw Scholar.*; *without blinking.*; *to rearrange the room.*; *Come home.*
6. **Names institutions precisely** (HKU GHD, St John’s, Laidlaw / Bishai, make_sense, WHS Berlin, Laureate Forum, MEDocGPT, GraphCast/Observatory, Qiskit/HKUST) — then **interprets** them. Lists alone feel unlike Bob; interpretation is the voice.
7. **Chinese register** (when required): formal 書面語, parallel structures (三個維度), policy lexicon (健康中國 2030, Health in All Policies, 跨界連接者), still anchored in lived Shanghai/Hong Kong imagery.

## Do not

- Lead with Shanghai-born / HK-raised identity politics when soft identity is constrained (see Tung & Ngai notes); show affection for Hong Kong and commitment through **work**.
- Invent current events, rankings, “highest GPA,” or unconfirmed awards.
- Dump achievements without a thesis sentence that metabolizes them.
- Over-compress into generic scholarshipese (“passionate leader passionate about passion”).
- Copy a sample verbatim when asked to write “better” — **steal structure and voice density**, upgrade argument and clarity.
- Put Tung & Ngai shortlist on materials until awarded.
- Flatten Chinese essays into translated English tone (or vice versa) without adjusting register.

## English micro-style

- Prefer concrete verbs: *crossed*, *held*, *witnessed*, *translated*, *rearranged*.
- Sentence rhythm: one long analytical sentence, then a short hit.
- Light British/HK academic spelling is fine when Bob uses it (*programme*, *endeavour*); stay consistent within a piece.
- Avoid emoji; avoid purple “AI brochure” diction.
