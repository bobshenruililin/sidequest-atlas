## 2. What are your reasons and expectations of joining this scheme?

**Paste-ready answer:**

I am applying to GLOCAL Connectors because I want to practise the work I already believe in: being a bridge that newcomers can actually use.

My degree in Global Health and Development trains me to move between systems — clinic and community, data and dignity. At HKU that same instinct shows up as support work: peer mentoring in HKUMed, coordinating a Wu Zhi Qiao student team after Gannan fieldwork, and helping host a St. John’s alumni exhibition. Those roles taught me that “welcome” is not a speech. It is accessibility — answering the small questions before they become loneliness, and making a room feel shared rather than merely open.

I expect to serve from August to April as someone reliable: reachable when a new non-local undergraduate needs a campus translation, present at gatherings rather than ornamental on a roster, and willing to help organise the ordinary rituals that turn arrival into belonging. I hope to refine how I listen across difference — language, expectation, homesickness — and to share a grounded version of HKU life: St. John’s courts and canteens, how to ask for help without shame, how Hong Kong’s density can feel both thrilling and exhausting.

My year has already taken me far — Forum rooms, Berlin, Mainland fieldwork. This scheme is how I bring that energy home. I do not want to be a guest between flights on my own campus. I want to be the person who meets someone at the beginning of their HKU story and stays long enough for the story to settle.
