# Cultural tours / exchanges / visits

**Prompt:** Please share your experiences in other cultural tours / exchange programmes / company visits / overseas internships etc. (if any)?

---

A passport measures distance traveled, but it fails to capture the distance the mind has stretched to accommodate new realities. My travels have been less about sightseeing and more about constructing new mental frameworks.

At the Asian Spring Programme on Rationality in Taiwan, I learned game theory and cognitive science with talented peers from around the world. We spent weeks dissecting human decision-making—training that fundamentally rewired how I process complex systems. Looking at Shanghai’s healthcare infrastructure, I see past the hospitals to the invisible architecture of incentives and logic that drives policy.

I thrive in the friction of cross-disciplinary work. As a Youth Fellow at the MIT Hong Kong Innovation Node, the challenge extended beyond engineering to the rapid-fire code-switching required to align entrepreneurs across the US and HK. I discovered that innovation ignites in the messy, loud overlap between differing perspectives. That is the energy I intend to bring to our exchanges with Fudan University students, and among ourselves.

This adaptability was forged in high-stakes environments. Undertaking a solo expedition to represent my discipline at the World Health Summit in Berlin was a test of both logistical independence and professional poise. It taught me the art of scientific diplomacy—maintaining the delicate balance of representing an institution with maturity while remaining open enough to absorb global best practices.

Finally, returning to Shanghai feels like a homecoming. Since I attended kindergarten there, the city’s rhythm feels like part of who I am. We have been separated for years, and I am eager to see how much we have both grown. I possess the cultural fluency to skip the acclimation phase and dive immediately into the nuance, bridging the gap between the Shanghai of my childhood memories and the medical metropolis it has become.
