# Internship / mentorship and Wuxi–Shanghai study tour (≤200 words each)

---

## What do you expect to learn from the Summer Internship Program and/or Mentorship Scheme? (not more than 200 words)

My driving ambition is to translate scientific breakthroughs into tangible benefits for human lives. I am applying to this program to learn the complete language of that process.

I want to witness firsthand how a research discovery becomes a viable startup, and how rigorous project management bridges the two. My goal is to understand these three areas not as separate functions, but as a single, dynamic pathway from lab to market. From the mentorship scheme, I seek the unwritten curriculum: the practical wisdom from leaders in entrepreneurship and healthcare who have navigated this path.

I expect to learn frameworks and skills. I hope to be surprised by the challenges and questions I haven't yet learned to anticipate. Ultimately, I want to learn how to build ventures that meaningfully improve people's health.

## Why do you want to join this Study Tour and what do you expect to learn from this Study Tour? (not more than 200 words)

As a Global Health and Development student at HKU, my academic focus is on acquiring "structural fluency"—understanding how macro-level policies and innovations translate into on-the-ground clinical realities. I want to join the Wuxi-Shanghai Study Tour because Wuxi represents the industrial and R&D engine of China's biopharma sector, while Shanghai serves as its global commercial hub.
Having grown up in Shanghai and now studying in Hong Kong, I am familiar with the clinical and financial nodes of healthcare. However, to truly understand East Asia’s tech-forward, rapidly aging health systems, I must examine the infrastructure that powers them. Wuxi’s concentration of life science parks offers a critical missing piece in my education: the innovation and manufacturing pipeline.
From this tour, I expect to learn the "governance grammar" of the Yangtze River Delta's biotech ecosystem. I want to observe how R&D scales into accessible treatments and how industry leaders navigate the tension between rapid commercialization and health equity. Ultimately, this experience will deepen my ability to bridge epidemiological data with actionable, cross-border health policy.
