# Nonacademic interest — photography (≤250 words)

**Prompt:** So that we can get to know a little bit more about you personally, please share a nonacademic interest or experience that is important to you. Why is it meaningful to you? (250 words)

---

My camera is my passport to the overlooked. While I grew up sketching, finding comfort in creating worlds from scratch on a blank page, I eventually traded my pencil for a shutter. The shift was philosophical: drawing was about invention, but photography is about witnessing. It forced me to stop imposing my imagination on the world and start respecting the reality before me.

Whether in the streets of São Paulo or the mountains of Tibet, the camera demands that I pay attention to the light, the texture, and the humanity that others might walk past. It serves as a bridge rather than a barrier. I recall a moment in rural Vietnam where a language gap seemed insurmountable. Yet, by gesturing to my lens and sharing the resulting image on the digital screen, a wary stranger transformed into a laughing friend.

I compile these moments into physical photobooks, adding written reflections to the visual data. This ritual is crucial. In a fast-paced life, it allows me to pause and recognize patterns in what I choose to preserve—often moments of resilience and quiet dignity. Photography grants me a particular, empathetic gaze, training me to see the human nuance behind the statistics I study. It is meaningful because it reminds me that to truly understand the world, one must first be willing to look at it closely, without blinking.
