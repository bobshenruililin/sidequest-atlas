# Why HKU (≤250 words)

**Prompt:** Please indicate the most influential factors in your decision to attend your present college (for example: location, cost, size of student body, special program offered, etc.) (250 words)

---

I chose the University of Hong Kong (HKU) because I needed a launchpad, not just a classroom. My decision was driven by a search for three specific elements: structural agility, intense community, and resource efficiency.

First, the Global Health and Development (GHD) program was the only major that promised structural fluency. Having thrived in the IBDP, I sought a curriculum that didn't force me to choose between science and sociology. GHD offered a rare interdisciplinary rigor, demanding I master epidemiology and economics simultaneously. I knew the field placements would force me out of the lecture hall and into the messy reality of healthcare, which is exactly where I learn best.

Second, St. John’s College was non-negotiable. I was wary of the social anonymity often found in large Asian research universities. St. John’s offered the antidote: a fiercely tight-knit, tradition-steeped brotherhood. I wanted an environment that would demand my participation—in sports, in governance, and in debate. I chose HKU knowing that St. John’s would provide the social anchor I needed to take risks elsewhere.

Finally, the value proposition was undeniable. As a local student, the subsidized tuition meant I could reallocate my resources toward fearless inquiry. Instead of drowning in debt, I could invest in travel to the World Health Summit in Berlin and fieldwork in Gannan. I chose HKU because it gave me the financial and academic freedom to start my career before I even graduated.
