# Wu Zhi Qiao course statement (≤250 words)

**Prompt:** why interested; qualifications; contribution areas. Mandarin programme note in original call.

---

Earlier this year, I joined an HKU Horizons trip to Gannan, a Tibetan region in Gansu. I watched a woman measure the road to Lhasa with her body. I met a caretaker who named his dog Buddha. I stood at a sky burial site and learned that stillness is not something I yet know how to hold.
I expected the trip to feel like enough. It wasn't — I crossed, and the crossing made me hungry, not full. That is why I am applying to Wu Zhi Qiao.
In Gannan, I observed. In Macha, I want to stay. Twelve days is long enough to learn someone's name and the story behind it. My academic work examines health policy across borders, but Gannan taught me that the most important translations happen between listening and understanding. I want to hold a shovel, sit with a villager's oral history, and discover what "sustainability" means to people who have practiced it for generations without needing the word.
I am qualified because Gannan changed what I think qualifications are. I have cooked with my grandmother's wok in Bazhao, conducted ethnographic fieldwork in Tibetan China, and learned — as a Laidlaw Scholar and basketball player — when to lead, when to pass, when to simply carry.
I can contribute through documentation: my camera, my bilingual fluency, my patience. I can bridge between Hong Kong and mainland students. And I can help ensure that when we leave Macha, something we built together stays behind.
